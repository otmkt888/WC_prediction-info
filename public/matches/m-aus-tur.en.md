## away_note

## summary_verdict

The presence of Arda Güler (Real Madrid), Hakan Çalhanoğlu (Inter Milan), and Ferdi Kadıoğlu (Brighton) as first-choice players in top European leagues gives Turkey a far superior overall technical level compared to Australia. Vincenzo Montella's mature 4-2-3-1 system delivered high consistency throughout qualifying — including a 2-2 draw against Spain — confirming that Turkey's pedigree is no illusion. The only real variable is Kenan Yıldız's fitness; if he is absent, Turkey's left-wing threat drops significantly, which represents Australia's best avenue for an upset.

Australia under Tony Popovic's 3-4-2-1 system are disciplined and well-organised defensively, expert at compressing space and launching rapid counter-attacks. Mohamed Touré's red-hot form and Nestory Irankunda (Bayern) coming off the bench as a wildcard option give Australia genuine attacking outlets. The full squad of 26 is fit and morale is high.

Prediction: Turkey 2-1. Turkey dominate midfield and break the deadlock through a Güler individual moment or a set piece; Australia score on the counter, but ultimately cannot match Turkey's overall squad depth and fall to a one-goal defeat. Should Yıldız recover and come on as a substitute, Turkey's path to victory becomes even more secure.
