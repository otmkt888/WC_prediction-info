## summary_verdict
Ivory Coast arrive with the confidence of AFCON champions and the momentum from a pre-tournament win over France. Their attack is formed by a twin-winger combination of Yan Diomandé (Bundesliga Best Newcomer) and Amad Diallo (Manchester United) — fast, creative, and dangerous. However, the absence of Haller weakens the central striking position and big-tournament experience is limited.

Ecuador are one of the hardest sides to beat in this tournament — unbeaten in 19 matches, conceding only 5 goals in qualification. Their defensive pairing of Pacho and Hincapié both featured in Champions League finals, and Caicedo's ability to control midfield is world-class. The weakness is a relatively blunt attack, relying on Valencia's veteran instincts or a flash of Páez's genius.

Prediction: 1-1 draw. Ecuador's defence is too solid for Ivory Coast to score more than once; but Ivory Coast's pace and creativity are enough to cancel out Ecuador's lead. A draw is the most logical outcome and a fair starting point for both sides' group-stage ambitions.
